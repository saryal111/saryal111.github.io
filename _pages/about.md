# Welcome to the MATRIX Lab
The MATRIX Lab — Membrane, Aerospace, and Transport for Integrated Systems — at the University of Maryland Eastern Shore explores the science of heat, water, and mass transport in engineered systems. Our research focuses on  membrane- based technologies, aerospace thermal management, aircraft environmental control systems, and controlled environment agriculture for Earth-based and space-based applications. By combining experiments, transport modeling, and system-level design, the lab develops sustainable technologies for efficient climate control, water management, and resource use in aircraft, greenhouses, and future space habitats.

Led by Dr. Sandeep Aryal, the MATRIX Lab works at the intersection of thermal sciences, heat and mass transfer, membrane-based separation, and aerospace systems. Our research focuses on understanding how heat, water, and chemical species move across interfaces, through membranes, and within engineered environments. This transport-focused approach connects our work across multiple applications, including waste heat recovery, air dehumidification and climate control, water harvesting and purification, mineral recovery, aircraft environmental control, and controlled environment agriculture for Earth and space. Across these areas, our goal is to design, model, and develop efficient, sustainable systems by controlling transport processes at the material, interface, and system levels.

At the MATRIX Lab, research and student development go hand in hand. We are dedicated to creating a supportive and rigorous research environment where students learn to ask meaningful questions, design experiments, build models, analyze data, and communicate scientific results. By working on problems in heat and mass transfer, membrane separations, aircraft climate control, water systems, and controlled environments for Earth and space, students gain practical research experience while contributing to technologies that address important societal and engineering challenges.
