The MATRIX Lab pursues research at the intersection of thermal sciences, heat and mass transfer, and membrane-based separation. Below are some of our ongoing and planned research directions.

## Liquid Desiccant Dehumidification for Controlled-Environment Agriculture: Thermodyamic study

<img src="/images/research1.png" alt="Liquid desiccant dehumidification" style="width:320px; float:right; margin:0 0 15px 20px; border-radius:8px;">

This ongoing project evaluates fertilizer-based liquid desiccants as consumable desiccants for energy-efficient greenhouse dehumidification by combining thermodynamic analysis and crop-specific mass balances to quantify dehumidification capacity and potential energy savings over conventional liquid desiccant systems.

<div style="clear:both;"></div>

## Freshwater Harvesting from Oceanic Water Vapor

<img src="/images/research2.png" alt="Ocean vapor harvesting" style="width:320px; float:right; margin:0 0 15px 20px; border-radius:8px;">

Vast quantities of freshwater exist as vapor in the air above the ocean surface. We study the capture-and-transport engineering needed to harvest that vapor — closing the gap between knowing the water is there and collecting it usefully — with a planned coastal field pilot on the Delmarva Peninsula.

<div style="clear:both;"></div>

## Selective Mineral Recovery from Desalination Brines

<img src="/images/research3.png" alt="Mineral recovery from brines" style="width:320px; float:right; margin:0 0 15px 20px; border-radius:8px;">

Desalination produces concentrated brine that is usually discarded as waste. We investigate how ions concentrate at membrane surfaces during reverse osmosis and how to selectively recover valuable minerals from that brine — turning a disposal problem into a resource-recovery opportunity.

<div style="clear:both;"></div>

## Waste-Heat Recovery and Water Extraction from Flue Gas

<img src="/images/research4.png" alt="Waste-heat recovery" style="width:320px; float:right; margin:0 0 15px 20px; border-radius:8px;">

We model low-temperature waste-heat recovery using organic Rankine cycle architectures that simultaneously recover heat and extract water from flue gas — improving efficiency and reclaiming water in thermal power systems.

<div style="clear:both;"></div>

## Membrane-Based Climate Control for Aircraft Cabins

<img src="/images/research5.png" alt="Aircraft cabin climate control" style="width:320px; float:right; margin:0 0 15px 20px; border-radius:8px;">

We explore membrane-based approaches to manage humidity and air quality in aircraft cabin environments. Selective membrane transport offers a lightweight, energy-efficient route to control moisture and maintain passenger comfort in the demanding conditions of flight, connecting membrane science with aerospace environmental control systems.
